## <span style="font-family:Arial;">Where I'm at Now</span>
I have successfully narrowed down all my income options to one. Sign up for my weekly newsletter to find out which one I chose! Just kidding. It's Amazon FBA. For those who are unfamiliar, FBA stands for Fulfilled by Amazon. So I will be selling on amazon using their fulfillment centers for housing inventory, boxing, labeling, and shipping instead of doing all of that out of my home. If that sounds like a scam, wait till you read the next paragraph.

I have always heard that if you want to do something right, get a mentor. So, I signed up for a skool community called [Amazon FBA | Completely Remote](https://www.skool.com/amazonfbaremote) which is $128 per month. The guy leading it is named Jay LaRosa and has built a business making over $2 million a year on Amazon. I know, I know... this sounds sketchy as heck. But he has proven himself to be legit while I was in his free community and I have come to really trust and respect him. ALSO, he promises a full refund after 3 months if members haven't hit $10,000 per month. So why not?

## <span style="font-family:Arial;">Where I'm Headed</span>
I have a close friend from Spain who always brings me back a chocolate spread (it's basically better nutella) that her village makes. I am running low and tried finding it on Amazon. No success. I found their website after some heavy digging and noticed they don't sell in the States. So, I hopped on the opportunity and contacted them to be their seller on Amazon. Great success! This means a lot of work is ahead to jump through countless hurdles. However, I like hurdles because it means less competition.

## <span style="font-family:Arial;">Where I Hope to End Up</span>
I have thrown myself completely into this focus of Amazon FBA and am optimistic that I'll be seeing returns soon. Hopefully in 3 months from now I'll be filling my progress tracker in by thousands of dollars every week. Or maybe it will stay negative... Either way, I'm excited to learn and fall on my face.
